## Primary Communication Channels

Utilize these designated platforms for official internal business. Using the wrong channel can lead to missed information or delayed approvals.

### Messenger (Meta)
The primary platform for day-to-day coordination and immediate updates.
* **Main Management Group Chat:** For discussions involving all managers, Executive Committee members, and key staff. Use this for broad announcements and general management topics.
* **Team-Specific Group Chats:** Includes the Team Manager, players, Head Varsity Manager, relevant VPs, and the Secretary. Use these for specific roster operations, scheduling, and immediate team needs.

### Email
Reserved for formal communication and "paper trails."
* Use email for official documentation, submitting monthly reports, and making formal requests.
* Always use email when communicating with the **Adviser**, **SADO**, **Facilities Office**, or external partners.

### Meetings
Regular management meetings and General Assemblies are crucial for decision-making. 
* Attendance and active participation are expected. 
* Refer to the C&B for protocols regarding meeting quorum and official minutes.

> **Pro-Tip on Channel Usage:** Prioritize the Group Chats. This ensures transparency and keeps everyone informed. **Avoid "sliding into the DMs"** of Executive Committee members or the Head Varsity Manager unless the matter is strictly confidential or an absolute emergency. Use the GCs to respect everyone’s time.

## Communication Etiquette

How you communicate is just as important as what you communicate.

* **Responsiveness:** Acknowledge messages and emails promptly. If you can't provide an answer immediately, a simple "Received, checking on this" goes a long way.
* **Clarity:** Be concise. In a fast-moving organization, clear and direct language prevents errors.
* **Respect:** Treat everyone—from the newest recruit to the Faculty Adviser—with respect. Constructive disagreement is healthy; toxicity is not.
* **Confidentiality:** Never share sensitive internal discussions outside of the authorized group chats or designated individuals.

## Reporting Structure & Updates

Information must flow correctly to ensure leadership can support your team effectively.

* **Team Status Updates:** If you are a Team Manager, provide regular updates to the **Head Varsity Manager** regarding your roster's progress, issues, and upcoming needs.
* **Smart Escalation:** Follow the reporting lines. Escalate conduct issues to the VPI/Head Manager and financial queries to the Treasurer.
* **Meeting Prep:** Don't come to meetings empty-handed. Prepare your team updates and data in advance so the session remains productive.
