description: At FIT ELITE, "student" always comes before "athlete." Academic success is paramount, and players must remain in good standing to participate. Your role as a manager is to ensure players excel in the classroom so they can keep competing in the server.
---

## Reinforcing Academic Requirements

Consistency is key. Players need to be reminded that their spot on the roster is tied to their performance in the classroom.

* **Minimum Eligibility:** Regularly remind players of the core requirements:
    * **Enrollment:** Must be a bona fide enrolled student at FEU Tech.
    * **Unit Load:** Must carry the minimum academic load (currently **12 units**).
    * **Good Standing:** Must maintain the academic and disciplinary standards set by the university.
* **The "Academics First" Rule:** Emphasize that academic responsibilities take precedence over any team activity. Players have the explicit right to prioritize their studies.

## Monitoring & Early Intervention

You aren't a professor, but you are a mentor. Being observant can help save a player's eligibility before it's too late.

* **Informal Check-ins:** Regularly ask players how they are balancing their workload. These don't need to be formal meetings—just a quick "How's the semester going?"
* **Spotting Red Flags:** Be attentive to signs of struggle, such as excessive stress, mention of failing grades, or a sudden drop-off in practice attendance.
* **Supportive Communication:** If you’re worried, discuss it privately with the **Head Varsity Manager** or the **Organizational Adviser**. 
    * *Note:* Respect player privacy. Do not demand to see grades unless a player volunteers them or it’s required for a specific process like an excuse letter.

## Connecting Players with University Resources

When a player is struggling, don't just tell them to "study harder"—show them where to get help.

* **Academic Support:** Guide players toward FEU Tech’s specialized resources:
    * **Tutoring Services & Academic Advising.**
    * **The iCare Program:** For library resources and specialized workshops.
    * **Writing Centers:** For help with papers and documentation.
* **Proactive Timing:** Mention these resources during high-stress periods like midterms and finals weeks.

## Managing Conflicts with Classes

Esports should complement the university experience, not hinder it.

* **The Excuse Letter Process:** You are the lead for facilitating official Excuse Letters. 
    * **Advance Notice:** Requests must be made well in advance of the event.
    * **Coordination:** Work with the Secretary and Vice Presidents to follow proper university procedures.
* **Mindful Scheduling:** When setting practice times, be mindful of the academic calendar. Avoid scheduling heavy practice blocks during exam weeks or major project deadlines.
