## Coordinating Practice Sessions

You handle the logistics of day-to-day training so the team can focus entirely on the game.

* **Establish the Routine:** Work closely with the Coach and Head Varsity Manager to set a consistent practice schedule that respects players' academic commitments.
* **Clear Communication:** Broadcast the confirmed schedule to all players and coaches well in advance. No one should ever have to ask, "When is practice today?"
* **Resource Readiness:** Ensure all necessary equipment or digital resources (e.g., lobby passwords, updated game clients, or physical room bookings) are ready for the session.

## Scheduling Scrimmages

Scrimmages (scrims) are the lifeblood of competitive growth. Managing these requires external networking and precise coordination.

* **Identify Opponents:** Proactively find potential scrimmage partners, such as other collegiate rosters or high-level amateur teams.
* **Negotiate Details:** Reach out to opposing managers to confirm:
    * **Technical Specs:** Game versions, server locations, and specific rulesets.
    * **Logistics:** Exact start times and the number of maps/rounds to be played.
* **Team Readiness:** Ensure your players are checked-in, warmed up, and ready to play 15 minutes before the scheduled start time.
* **Progress Tracking:** Maintain a record of all scrimmage schedules and results to help the coaching staff track team development over time.
