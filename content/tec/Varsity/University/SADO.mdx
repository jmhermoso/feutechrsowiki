## SADO (Student Activities & Development Office)

SADO is your primary point of contact for administrative oversight, approvals, and guidance. They are the bridge between our student organization and the university administration.

### Tournament, Event, & Activity Approvals
Almost every official FIT ELITE activity—from high-stakes tournaments to general workshops—requires SADO's green light.

* **Proposal Submission:** Collaborate with the Head Varsity Manager or the Executive Committee to prepare detailed event proposals or activity request forms.
* **Lead Time is Critical:** SADO operates on strict deadlines. Always submit your proposals well in advance of your planned event date to avoid rejection due to timing.
* **Internal Endorsement:** Before a proposal hits SADO's desk, it must be reviewed and endorsed by the Secretariat and the Vice Presidents.
* **Policy Compliance:** Every activity must align with both university-wide policies and specific SADO guidelines.

### Official Communication & Liaison
SADO acts as the official channel for formal university interactions.

* **Policy Clarification:** If you are unsure how a university policy impacts an esports event, consult SADO or the Organizational Adviser for an official interpretation.
* **Official Requests:** Any request requiring university-level approval (beyond basic room bookings) must be routed through SADO.
* **Eligibility Verification:** SADO is the final authority that officially verifies and approves player rosters for tournament participation. If SADO hasn't cleared a player, they don't play.
