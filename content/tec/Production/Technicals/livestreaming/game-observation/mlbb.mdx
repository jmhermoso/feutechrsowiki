# Mobile Legends: Bang Bang

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB) is a 5v5 mobile MOBA. Two teams compete to destroy the opposing team's **Base** (the central structure in their territory). The map is divided into three lanes (Top, Mid, Bottom) connected by a jungle. Matches are won by taking objectives, controlling the map, and eventually breaking the enemy base.

## Map Structure

| Area | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Top Lane** | The upper lane connecting both bases |
| **Mid Lane** | The central lane — shortest path between bases, high traffic |
| **Bottom Lane** | The lower lane, typically where the Gold Laner farms |
| **Jungle** | The area between lanes containing neutral monsters and objectives |
| **Lord Pit** | The upper jungle area where the Lord spawns |
| **Turtle Pit** | The lower jungle area where the Turtle spawns |
| **Base** | Each team's main structure — destroying it wins the game |
| **Turrets** | Defensive structures protecting each lane — must be destroyed to advance |

## Key Objectives

<Tabs items={['Lord', 'Turtle', 'Turrets and Base']}>
  <Tab value="Lord">
    The Lord is the most powerful neutral objective in the game. Killing it grants the team a powerful allied siege unit that pushes a lane toward the enemy base.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Lord spawn** | Lord first spawns at approximately 10 minutes into the match |
    | **Lord pit** | The location in the upper jungle where Lord spawns |
    | **Contesting Lord** | When both teams fight at the Lord pit simultaneously |
    | **Securing Lord** | Landing the killing blow on Lord to claim it for your team |
    | **Lord push** | The Lord sieging a lane after being secured — often ends the game |

    **Observer priority:** Lord fights are the highest-stakes moments in most MLBB games. As soon as teams begin moving toward Lord, position the camera at the pit entrance. Show both teams' approach, the fight, and the securing moment. If Lord is secured, cut to the lane it's pushing to show the siege.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Turtle">
    The Turtle is a lesser neutral objective that grants gold and experience to the team that kills it.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Turtle spawn** | Spawns at approximately 2 minutes, respawns every 2 minutes |
    | **Turtle pit** | Located in the lower jungle |
    | **Contesting Turtle** | Both teams fighting at or near the Turtle pit |

    **Observer priority:** Early Turtle fights establish map control and should be followed. Later in the game, Turtle becomes secondary to Lord — only cut to it if a fight develops around it.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Turrets and Base">
    Turrets are the primary strategic objective beyond neutral monsters. Destroying turrets opens lanes for pushing and eventually exposes the base.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Tower dive** | Attacking an enemy player while they are under the protection of their turret |
    | **Outer turret** | The first turret in each lane — typically falls in the early-mid game |
    | **Inner turret** | The second turret, closer to the base — falling here opens a direct push path |
    | **Base turret / Inhibitor turret** | The final turret before the base crystal |
    | **Crystal / Nexus** | The core structure — destroying it ends the game |
    | **Base race** | Both teams ignoring defense to attack each other's base simultaneously |

    **Observer priority:** Tower dives are high-risk, high-reward moments — follow them. A base race is the most dramatic possible ending — cut wide to show both bases under attack simultaneously if possible.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Role Callouts

| Role | Function | Observer Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gold Laner** | Farms the bottom lane for gold; primary damage dealer late game | Follow in late-game teamfights — they deal the most damage |
| **EXP Laner** | Farms the top lane for experience; typically a durable fighter | Follow in side lane pressure situations |
| **Jungler / Assassin** | Clears jungle camps and ganks lanes | Follow when they rotate to a lane — a successful gank is a broadcast moment |
| **Midlaner / Mage** | Controls the mid lane and provides burst damage in teamfights | Follow during teamfight initiation |
| **Roamer / Support** | Supports allies, initiates fights, protects carries | Follow when they initiate a teamfight — their action sets the fight in motion |

<Callout type="info" title="Follow the Jungler in early game">
  The Jungler's rotation pattern determines which lane gets pressure first. In the early game, tracking the Jungler tells the observer where the first significant fight will happen.
</Callout>

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="What is the most important moment to be on camera for in an MLBB match?">
    Lord fight, in almost every case. A won Lord fight frequently leads directly to the end of the game. If both teams are moving toward Lord, nothing else on the map matters more — commit to that camera position.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I follow a teamfight that spans multiple lanes?">
    Start with the widest angle that still shows the fight. As the fight resolves to a smaller number of players, move to closer, more dramatic angles. If the fight splits (one team retreats, another pushes), follow the team on the offensive.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
