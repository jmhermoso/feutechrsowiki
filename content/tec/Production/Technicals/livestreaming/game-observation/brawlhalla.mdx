# Brawlhalla

Brawlhalla is a free-to-play platform fighter developed by Blue Mammoth Games. Players fight on floating platforms and attempt to knock opponents off the stage and out of bounds. Matches are played in 1v1 or 2v2 formats, with stock (life) counts determining the winner.

## Match Structure

| Term | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Stock** | A life — each player starts with a set number of stocks (typically 3) |
| **KO** | Knocking an opponent off the stage beyond the blast zone — they lose one stock |
| **Blast zone** | The invisible boundary surrounding the stage — being knocked past it results in a KO |
| **Respawn** | After losing a stock, the player returns to the stage from above |
| **Last stock** | When a player is on their final life — any KO ends their match |
| **Set** | The full match — typically first to eliminate all opponent stocks |

## Key Mechanics and Callouts

<Tabs items={['Damage and Knockback', 'Offstage and Edgeguarding', 'Weapons and Signatures', '2v2 Format']}>
  <Tab value="Damage and Knockback">
    Unlike most fighting games, Brawlhalla does not use health bars. Instead, players accumulate damage (shown as a percentage) which increases the knockback of attacks — the higher the damage, the farther a hit sends the opponent.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Damage percentage** | Accumulated damage — higher percentage means attacks send the opponent farther |
    | **Knockback** | The force and direction of an attack's impact — scales with damage percentage |
    | **True combo** | A sequence of attacks that cannot be escaped — guaranteed damage |
    | **Chase** | Following an opponent offstage to attempt an edgeguard |
    | **Reset** | Returning to neutral position after an exchange |
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Offstage and Edgeguarding">
    Edgeguarding is the practice of preventing an opponent from returning to the stage after being knocked off. It is one of the highest-skill expressions in Brawlhalla and a primary source of broadcast moments.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Edgeguard** | Attacking an opponent while they attempt to return to the stage |
    | **Offstage** | The area beyond the stage platform — recovery is required to return |
    | **Recovery** | The player's attempt to return to the stage — typically using a jump and then a directional or dodge option |
    | **Gimp** | KOing an opponent at low damage percentage by hitting them offstage before they can recover |
    | **Ledge / Platform** | Stage structures used during recovery and edgeguarding sequences |
    | **Spike** | An attack that sends the opponent downward at a steep angle — extremely difficult to recover from |

    **Observer priority:** Offstage sequences are the most dramatic moments in Brawlhalla. Follow the action off the stage — do not stay anchored to the platform when the fight goes offstage. A successful gimp or spike at low percentage is a crowd moment.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Weapons and Signatures">
    Each Legend (character) in Brawlhalla uses up to two weapon types. Weapons spawn on the stage and can be picked up by either player.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Legend** | A playable character in Brawlhalla — each has a unique Signature set and two weapons |
    | **Weapon** | A tool that spawns on stage — grants new attack options when held (e.g. Sword, Axe, Scythe, Blasters) |
    | **Unarmed** | Fighting without a weapon — faster but lower knockback |
    | **Signature (Sig)** | Each Legend's unique special move — performed with a specific weapon, deals high knockback |
    | **Neutral Sig** | A signature performed from a standing position |
    | **Side Sig** | A signature performed while moving horizontally |
    | **Down Sig** | A signature performed while holding down |
    | **Weapon toss** | Throwing a held weapon at the opponent as a projectile |

    **Observer priority:** Signature moves are the highest-knockback attacks in the game and are often the KO tool. When a player sets up a Signature read, that exchange is the broadcast moment — zoom in or cut to show the result clearly.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="2v2 Format">
    In 2v2, two teams of two compete with shared stock pools. Partners can interact — accidentally hitting teammates is possible.

    | Term | Meaning |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Team stock** | The combined stock count for both players on a team |
    | **Friendly fire** | Attacking a teammate — usually accidental but can affect the match |
    | **2v1** | When one team has both players alive against one remaining opponent |
    | **Carry** | One teammate performing significantly better than the other |

    **Observer priorities in 2v2:**
    - Track the **team stock counts** — when one team is down a stock, the balance of the fight changes
    - A **2v1 situation** is a broadcast moment — follow the outnumbered player's survival attempt
    - **Partner coordination** (simultaneous attacks, covering recovery) is the skill expression specific to 2v2 — show it when it happens
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Should I follow the fight offstage or stay on the main platform?">
    Follow the fight offstage. The most dramatic moments in Brawlhalla happen in the offstage space — recoveries, edgeguards, spikes, and gimps. Staying on the main platform during an offstage exchange means missing the story. Return to the platform view when both players reset to neutral.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I track two fights at once in 2v2?">
    Prioritize the fight closest to a KO — track whoever is at the highest damage percentage or most vulnerable off-stage. If both fights are at similar intensity, pull back to a wider angle that shows both exchanges. A full-team wipe (both opponents KO'd simultaneously) is rare but worth waiting for a wide angle.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
