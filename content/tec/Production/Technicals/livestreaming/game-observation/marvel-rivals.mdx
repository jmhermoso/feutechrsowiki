# Marvel Rivals

Marvel Rivals is a 6v6 hero shooter developed by NetEase Games. Two teams of six players — each playing a Marvel character with unique abilities — compete across objective-based maps. Matches are decided by controlling or escorting a payload, capturing a point, or a combination of both depending on the map.

## Game Modes

<Tabs items={['Convoy (Escort)', 'Domination (Control Point)', 'Convergence (Hybrid)']}>
  <Tab value="Convoy (Escort)">
    One team escorts a payload along a track while the other defends. The payload moves when attackers are near it and stops when defenders contest it.

    **Observer priorities:**
    - Keep the **payload in frame** or clearly implied by context — viewers must always know where the objective is
    - Watch for **team fight initiation** near the payload — this is where rounds are won and lost
    - Cut to **wide shots** when both teams are fully engaged on the payload
    - Cut to **player POV** during a flank or a high-value elimination
    - Track the **payload progress percentage** — when it reaches a checkpoint, expect a reset fight
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Domination (Control Point)">
    Teams fight to capture and hold a single point in the center of the map. The first team to reach the capture percentage threshold wins the round.

    **Observer priorities:**
    - The **control point** is always the primary focus — frame it and the fight around it
    - Watch for **ultimate ability usage** — in Domination, team ultimates often decide the hold or the take
    - Cut to **aerial or elevated angles** when possible to show both teams' positioning around the point
    - Track **capture percentage** — when one team is close to capping, the defending team will commit fully — anticipate the fight
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Convergence (Hybrid)">
    A combination of Escort and Domination — attackers must first capture a point, then escort a payload.

    **Observer priorities:**
    - Phase 1 (capture): Treat as Domination — focus on the point and team positioning
    - Phase 2 (escort): Treat as Convoy — keep the payload in context
    - The **transition moment** (point captured, payload unlocked) is a high-energy moment — be ready to cut wide to capture the full team's reaction and position
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Key Callouts and Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Payload** | The objective cart being escorted in Convoy/Convergence modes |
| **Control Point / CP** | The capture zone in Domination mode |
| **Ultimate / Ult** | Each hero's most powerful ability, charged over time by dealing or receiving damage |
| **Ult advantage** | When one team has more ultimates available than the other — a decisive strategic advantage |
| **Team fight** | An engagement where all or most of both teams are committed to the same fight |
| **Flank** | A player attacking from an unexpected angle, away from the main team fight |
| **Dive** | An aggressive push where heroes with mobility abilities close distance quickly to target backline players |
| **Peel** | Defenders turning to protect a teammate being dived |
| **Stagger** | When players on a team die at different times rather than together, making it impossible to mount a coordinated counter |
| **Full hold** | Defenders completely prevent the payload from moving or the point from being captured in a round |
| **Last fight** | The final engagement of a round — one team is about to win or lose based on the outcome |

## Observer Priority Guide

| Situation | Where to Look | Why |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Team fight on objective** | Wide shot, both teams visible | Shows positioning and numbers advantage |
| **Ultimate ability activating** | Cut to the hero using the ult | High-impact moment, visually dramatic |
| **1v1 duel off-objective** | Player POV or close follow | Highlights mechanical skill |
| **Payload nearly capped / point nearly taken** | Keep objective in frame | Tension — viewers need to see the progress |
| **Flank in progress** | Follow the flanker | Narrative — will the flank succeed? |
| **Respawn wave incoming** | Hold current fight angle | The respawn wave changes the fight — show the moment it arrives |

<Callout type="info" title="Track ultimate economy">
  Knowing which team has ultimates available before a fight breaks out tells you where the decisive moment will happen. Watch the ult charge indicators and anticipate, not react.
</Callout>

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="How do I know when a team fight is about to start?">
    Watch for the teams converging toward the objective, a player using a mobility ability to engage, or a support player activating a defensive cooldown. In most cases, the attacking team commits before the defending team fully responds — lead the action, don't follow it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Should I follow a player who is flanking away from the main fight?">
    Only if the flank is clearly high-value (targeting a key backline player, about to secure a team wipe, or happening during a moment of downtime in the main fight). Cutting away from the main fight for a minor flank that doesn't connect loses the narrative thread. Use judgment — if the flank is the story, follow it.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
