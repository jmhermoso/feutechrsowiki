# Game Observation

The Game Observer is one of the most important roles in an esports broadcast. Their job is to act as the eyes of the audience — anticipating action, following narrative, and framing the game in a way that makes it understandable and exciting for viewers who may not know the title deeply.

Observation is not passive. The observer must understand the game well enough to know where the next important moment will happen before it happens, and move the camera there in advance.

<Callout type="info" title="Terminology is game-specific">
  The same word means different things in different games. "Spike" in Valorant is the bomb. "Spike" in another title may refer to an ability or a character. Always refer to the correct game's page for callout terminology — do not carry assumptions across titles.
</Callout>

## How the Observer Works with the TD

The TD and Observer communicate over comms during the broadcast. The TD calls for specific angles based on the production runsheet and the flow of the match. The Observer executes those calls and also proactively calls out incoming moments the TD may not have visibility on from the encoder station.

**Standard callout pattern:**
- **TD to Observer:** "Eyes on Baron" / "Cut to player cam" / "Wide shot for the fight"
- **Observer to TD:** "Teamfight breaking out mid, ready for wide" / "Objective spawning in 10 seconds"

Each title's page documents what specific callouts mean in that game's context, what the key objectives are, and where the observer's attention should be at each phase of the match.
