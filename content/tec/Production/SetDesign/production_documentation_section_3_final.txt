# Section 3: Set Design and Fabrication

This section governs the physical environment of our productions. Our fabrication philosophy is **Student-First** and **Environmentally Conscious**, prioritizing accessible skill-building, lightweight logistics, and the creative reuse of materials.

---

## 3.1 Pre-Phase: Design and Budget

The pre-phase is a cyclical process of aligning creative ambition with financial and physical reality. No fabrication begins until the design is approved and the budget is finalized.

### Initial Brief and Creative Concept
The process starts with the **Client Brief**, where the desired "look" is established. The design team then creates **Sketches** and **Digital Renders** to visualize lighting, textures, and depth.
* **Student-First Design:** Renders should be achievable with basic hand tools. Avoid complex joinery that requires specialized industrial machinery.
* **Camera Testing:** Renders must account for camera angles (e.g., ensuring the backdrop is wide enough to cover a Medium-Wide shot).

### The Approval and Revision Loop
Set design is rarely approved on the first draft. The team must navigate a specific revision cycle:
1. **Initial Rendering:** Present the digital mockup and estimated costs.
2. **Review:** Client provides feedback on aesthetics or budget constraints.
3. **Revision:** Adjust materials or design complexity to meet constraints.
4. **Final Sign-off:** No materials are purchased until the "Final Render" and "Cost Sheet" are signed.

---

## 3.2 Fabrication: The Build

Fabrication is the transition from digital pixels to physical matter. In our organization, we view the build as a learning opportunity for officers to gain practical engineering skills.

### Sustainable Building Ideology
We prioritize the **Circular Economy** in our fabrication process. Instead of buying new for every event, we focus on:
* **Recycled Materials:** Utilizing reclaimed wood pallets, cardboard for mockups, and repurposing plastic scraps for diffusers.
* **Modular "LEGO" Systems:** Building frames in standard sizes (e.g., 4ft x 8ft flats) that can be reconfigured and repainted for multiple years.
* **Low-Impact Finishes:** Favoring water-based paints and mechanical fasteners (screws/bolts) over permanent adhesives to allow for easy disassembly and recycling.

### Blueprinting and Assembly Guides
Digital renders are not blueprints. The fabrication lead must create detailed **Technical Drawings** showing exact dimensions.
* **Joinery:** Specify "Dry-Fit" methods first. Use bolts and wingnuts where possible so students can assemble and disassemble the set without power tools on-site.
* **Cable Paths:** Blueprints must include "hidden" channels for power and signal cables so they do not clutter the set on-camera.

---

## 3.3 Setup and Strike

The final phase involves the logistics of moving the set into the venue and properly decommissioning it after the event.

### Installation and Cable Management
On-site setup is where the "Set" and "Technicals" meet. 
* **Placement:** Position the set according to the venue scout notes. 
* **Cable Hiding:** Use gaff tape and "beauty panels" to hide all wiring. On camera, no loose cables should be visible.
* **The Camera Test:** View the set through the actual production cameras to check for "hot spots" (reflections) or shadows that need adjustment.

### Strike and Storage
A set is an investment of both money and carbon. The **Strike Phase** must be as systematic as the build.
* **"Deconstruction, Not Destruction":** Carefully remove screws and save them in labeled bins. Avoid "smashing" sets, as it ruins the material for future student projects.
* **Designated Storage:** Large set pieces must be labeled and stored in a dry environment to prevent warping.

---

### 🟢 Fabrication FAQ
> **What is the best "lightweight" material for backdrops?**
> **Coroplast (Corrugated Plastic)** is highly recommended. It is light enough for one student to carry, waterproof, and can be easily recycled after use.
>
> **How do we handle set pieces that are too heavy for the crew to move?**
> This is a design failure. All set pieces should be "Man-Portable" (movable by 2 students) or built on heavy-duty casters with locks.
