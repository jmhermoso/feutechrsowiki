## Lighting Types Reference

Lighting in a production set context falls into four functional categories. What fixtures are physically available depends on inventory or what can be requested — the descriptions below tell you what each type does so you know what to ask for.

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="PAR (Parabolic Aluminized Reflector)">
    PAR fixtures produce a focused, punchy beam of light with a distinct oval pool. They are common for stage wash and key lighting.

    - **Best for:** Washing a backdrop with color, creating dramatic key or rim lighting, stage fill
    - **Common variants:** PAR 64 (large, high-output), PAR 56, LED PAR (lower heat, color-mixing)
    - **Watch out for:** Hard-edged spill onto set pieces outside the beam — use barn doors or flags to control it
    - **On camera:** Can create hot spots on reflective surfaces; check during the Camera Test
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="LED Tubes (Wand / Neon-style)">
    LED tubes are long, linear fixtures that produce soft, even light along their length. They are increasingly common in production sets for their portability and color control.

    - **Best for:** Practical set elements (visible in-frame as a design feature), soft fill light, rim lighting on subjects
    - **Common variants:** RGB LED wands, bi-color LED batons, neon-flex strips
    - **Watch out for:** Color fringing or banding when shot at certain shutter speeds — test your camera settings before committing
    - **On camera:** Often used intentionally as a visible set element; confirm with the director whether the fixture should be in or out of frame
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Spot / Fresnel">
    Spot and Fresnel fixtures produce a controllable, focused beam with soft or hard edges depending on the lens focus. The Fresnel lens allows the beam spread to be adjusted by moving the lamp relative to the reflector.

    - **Best for:** Key lighting on a subject, logo or signage illumination, gobo projection onto backdrops
    - **Common variants:** Fresnel (adjustable beam), ERS / Leko (sharp-edged, accepts gobos), LED spot
    - **Watch out for:** Fresnel spill is wider than it appears — use barn doors and check the frame edges
    - **On camera:** Clean, controllable — preferred for interview-style or formal setups where a precise look is needed
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ambient / Soft Light">
    Ambient fixtures diffuse light broadly with no strong directionality. They fill shadows and raise the overall exposure of the set without creating a distinct look.

    - **Best for:** General set fill, reducing harsh shadows from key lights, portrait softboxes
    - **Common variants:** Softbox, LED panel with diffusion, umbrella reflector, practical lamps (floor lamps, fairy lights as set dressing)
    - **Watch out for:** Too much ambient fill flattens depth and dimension on camera — use sparingly and balance against your key light
    - **On camera:** Practical lamps used as set dressing must be checked for exposure — a lit lamp in frame can blow out if the scene is exposed for the subject
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>

## Fabrication Consumables Checklist

Before every build, confirm these consumables are stocked. Run low? Submit a replenishment request to the Logistics Officer before fabrication begins — not during.

| Item | Purpose | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gaff tape** | Cable management, set dressing, temporary fastening | Matte finish only — do not substitute with duct tape on set |
| **Painter's tape** | Masking edges before painting | Remove before paint fully cures to avoid peeling |
| **Water-based paint** | Surface finishing on set pieces | Check color accuracy under production lights, not just room light |
| **Plastic-adhesion primer** | Priming Coroplast or plastic surfaces before paint | Required for paint adhesion on smooth plastic |
| **Screws and bolts** | Structural fastening | Stock M6 bolts with wingnuts for tool-free on-site assembly |
| **Zip ties** | Cable routing and lightweight securing | Single-use — cut cleanly during strike, do not reuse |
| **Safety cables** | Overhead fixture backup retention | Mandatory for any suspended set piece or light |
