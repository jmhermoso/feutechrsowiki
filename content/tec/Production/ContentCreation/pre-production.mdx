# Phase 2 — Pre-Production: The Blueprint

Pre-production converts abstract ideas into **actionable technical data**. Without a blueprint, on-site execution becomes reactive rather than proactive. The more thoroughly this phase is completed, the fewer surprises on shoot day.

<Callout type="info" title="Entry Requirement">
  Pre-Production only begins after all **Pre-Pre deliverables** have been signed off. Starting this phase with an unresolved scope or unconfirmed SPOC cascades into larger problems downstream.
</Callout>

---

## Creative Assets

The team produces three sequential deliverables — Logline, Moodboard, and Storyboard. Each must be client-approved before the next begins.

<Tabs items={['Logline', 'Moodboarding', 'Storyboarding']}>
  <Tab value="Logline">
    ### The Logline — 1 Sentence

    A single sentence that keeps the narrative laser-focused. If the video cannot be described in one sentence, the concept is not clear enough yet.

    | ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | "A video about our esports org" | "A cinematic profile of TEC's competitive team as they prepare for their season-opening LAN" |
    | "Something hype for the event" | "A 60-second energy reel capturing the chaos and precision of TEC's event day — from setup to final buzzer" |

    **Writing Tips:**
    - Answer: "What is the emotional journey we're taking the viewer on?"
    - Use active verbs: following, discovering, building, revealing, competing
    - Keep it under 20 words
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Moodboarding">
    ### Moodboarding — 8–10 Reference Images

    Translates the client's vague "vibe" into specific, measurable targets the team can hit on-set.

    **How to Build It:**
    1. Collect images from Unsplash, Pinterest, or reference films and other esports creators
    2. Organize by theme: "Dark & cinematic" vs. "Bright & energetic"
    3. Annotate each image: "This lighting ratio", "This color grade", "This framing"

    **Deliverable Format:**
    - Shared Pinterest board or Figma file — not just chat links
    - Minimum 8 images with written notes on each
    - Formally shared with client for sign-off before storyboarding begins
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Storyboarding">
    ### Storyboarding — Shot-by-Shot Sequence

    A sequential map of every planned shot, bridging the moodboard and the final shotlist.

    **Panel Requirements:**
    - Shot number, shot type (W / M / CU / ECU), camera movement (Static / Pan / Dolly / Handheld)
    - Brief action description (1–2 sentences)
    - Estimated duration

    **Format:** Digital (Figma, Storyboarder) or physical sketches — stick figures are fine. Clarity over artistry.

    **Minimum:** 8 panels for any project. Multi-scene productions require a full panel per scene.

    **Client Approval:** Storyboard must be signed off before the Equipment Manifest is finalized. Changes now are free. Changes on set are not.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Creative Assets Checklist

- [ ] Logline written and approved by client
- [ ] Moodboard (8+ images, annotated) shared and signed off
- [ ] Storyboard with shot types, movements, and descriptions complete
- [ ] All assets uploaded to shared folder
- [ ] Client feedback incorporated before proceeding

## Logistics and Planning

### Venue Scout

A venue scout is mandatory — even for locations the team has used before. Facilities change: outlets fail, furniture obstructs prior angles, and clients modify spaces without notice. Always verify 24–48 hours prior.

**Document During the Scout:**
- Primary camera position (wide establishing shot)
- Secondary angles (medium, close-up, profile)
- Audio recording positions (loudest and quietest ambient zones)
- Talent traffic flow (where will talent stand, walk, sit?)
- Power outlet locations and circuit capacity
- Cable run paths and potential tripping hazards
- Emergency exit locations

<Callout type="warn" title="Scout Rule">
  Take **photos or short video clips at each camera position** during the scout. These go into the Pre-Production package and serve as reference for the Tech Lead during setup.
</Callout>

### Talent Coordination

Run talent sourcing in parallel with the venue scout to avoid timeline bottlenecks.

| Task | Timing |
| :--- | :--- |
| Confirm on-camera talent availability | 2 weeks before shoot |
| Send wardrobe brief | 1 week before shoot |
| Confirm call time, location, and parking | 48 hours before shoot |
| Day-before confirmation | 24 hours before shoot |

**Wardrobe Brief Tips:**
- Specify colors that work with the set: "Earth tones, no white or black solids"
- Specify fit: "Layers, movement-friendly, avoid logos conflicting with client branding"
- Always include a backup wardrobe option

## Equipment Manifest

The Equipment Manifest is the final output of Pre-Production — an itemized checklist of every component required for the shoot.

| Category | Item | Qty | Spec | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Camera** | Primary Body | 1 | As available | A-camera |
| **Lens** | 24–70mm | 2 | f/2.8 | A + B cam |
| **Audio** | Wireless Lav Set | 3 | 2.4 GHz | Talent + boom |
| **Power** | Battery Pack | 6 | Per camera spec | 8hr shoot needs 4 min |
| **Storage** | Cards / SSD | 8 | 128GB min | Verify write speed |
| **Lighting** | Key + Fill | 2 | As available | Check diffusion |
| **Grip** | Tripod, C-stand | Per shot plan | — | Include sandbags |

<Callout type="info" title="Manifest Timing">
  Create the manifest **2 weeks before the shoot**. Order any rentals immediately. Check every item **48 hours prior** — test batteries, verify lenses focus, confirm wireless audio has no interference at the location.
</Callout>

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Why do we need a moodboard if the client already gave us a reference video?">
    A reference video shows what someone else did. A moodboard shows what we will do. It translates the client's vague "vibe" into specific, measurable targets the team can hit during the shoot — for example, "Key light at 45°, warm color grade, shallow depth of field." It also surfaces disagreements early, when they're easy and free to fix.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is a venue scout necessary for a location we've used before?">
    Yes. Facilities change — power outlets may be broken, new furniture may obstruct previous angles, or the client may have modified the space. A 30-minute scout prevents a 3-hour problem on shoot day.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the client rejects the storyboard?">
    Document their feedback, revise, and resubmit. This is the least expensive time to make changes. Changes on set cost time and crew morale. Changes in post cost more. Changes after publication cost everything.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How detailed does the storyboard need to be?">
    Stick figures and rough shapes are acceptable. Every panel should answer three questions: Where is the camera? What is in frame? What is happening? If a crew member can look at a panel and understand what to set up, the panel is good enough.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
