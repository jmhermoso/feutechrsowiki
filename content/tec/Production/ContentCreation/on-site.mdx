# Phase 3 — On-Site: The Shoot

The on-site phase is characterized by **strict adherence to the schedule** and the physical safety of crew and equipment. Every decision made in Pre-Pre and Pre-Production exists to make this phase as smooth as possible.

<Callout type="info" title="Entry Requirement">
  The **Callsheet** and **Shotlist** must be printed and distributed to the full crew at least **24 hours before the shoot**. A crew that doesn't know the plan is a crew that improvises — and improvisation costs time.
</Callout>

---

## Production Documents

Two documents govern the shoot day: the **Callsheet** (logistics) and the **Shotlist** (creative operations).

<Tabs items={['Callsheet', 'Shotlist']}>
  <Tab value="Callsheet">
    ### The Callsheet — Logistics Heart

    The single source of truth for the shoot day. Every crew member and the client SPOC should have a copy.

    ```
    PRODUCTION: [Project Name]
    SHOOT DATE: [Date]
    CALL TIME: [Crew] | [Talent — typically 1 hr later]
    LOCATION: [Venue name]
      Address: [Full address]
      GPS: [Coordinates]
      Parking: [Specific instructions]

    WEATHER: [Forecast — note heat, humidity, rain risk]
    CREW: [Total count + attached roster]
    TALENT: [Name + talent brief reference]

    EMERGENCY CONTACTS:
      Location Manager: [Name + number]
      Nearest Medical: [Facility + estimated travel time]
      Client SPOC: [Name + number]
      Production Lead: [Name + number]

    EQUIPMENT: [Brief summary — camera count, audio, lighting]
    EXPECTED WRAP: [Time]
    ```

    **TEC Note:** For on-campus shoots, include the FEU Tech security contact and room/floor access procedures. For off-campus shoots, confirm the venue's emergency protocol in advance.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Shotlist">
    ### The Shotlist — Operational Heart

    A numbered sequence of every shot required to deliver the brief, prioritized so the team knows what to protect if time runs short.

    **Priority System:**
    - 🔴 **Must Have** — Project fails without this. Do not wrap until captured.
    - 🟡 **Nice to Have** — Strengthens the edit. Attempt after Must Haves are secured.
    - 🟢 **Bonus** — Extra coverage or social content. Only if time allows.

    | # | Shot | Description | Duration | Priority |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | 1 | Wide Establish | Subject enters frame, acknowledges camera | 8–10 sec | 🔴 Must Have |
    | 2 | Medium Close | Subject delivers key message to camera | 15–20 sec | 🔴 Must Have |
    | 3 | Subject Detail | Tight or macro shot of key focus | 10–15 sec | 🔴 Must Have |
    | 4 | B-Roll: Environment | Subject moves through the space naturally | 15–20 sec | 🟡 Nice to Have |
    | 5 | Interview Insert | Subject answers 3–5 directed questions | 30–45 sec | 🟢 Bonus |

    **Rule:** Complete all 🔴 Must Have shots before moving down the priority list. If running behind schedule, drop from the bottom up.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Run-of-Show

The Run-of-Show ensures technical foundations are laid before creative talent arrives. Talent waiting on technical setup wastes time and damages working relationships.

| Time | Phase | Activity | Lead |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| T+0 | **Load-In** | Equipment arrives, full inventory check, staging | Gear Tech |
| T+45 | **Tech Setup** | Power runs, cable routing, camera rigs, audio check | Tech Lead |
| T+60 | **Talent Arrival** | Greet talent, lav mic fitting, wardrobe final check | AD |
| T+90 | **Rehearsal** | Walk-through blocking, camera framing confirmation | Director |
| T+120 | **Rolling** | Record takes per shotlist, prioritize 🔴 Must Haves | Ops Lead |
| T+270 | **First Break** | 15–30 min — snack + water for crew and talent | — |
| T+300 | **Resume** | B-roll and bonus content per priority | Ops Lead |
| T+420 | **Lunch** | 60 min — never skip or compress | — |
| T+480 | **Final Shots** | Pick-ups, reshoots, flagged Must Haves | Director |
| T+570 | **Strike** | Systematic teardown, full gear inventory | Gear Tech |
| T+630 | **Wrap** | Final site check, talent release, crew sign-off | AD |

<Callout type="warn" title="Strike Is Not Optional">
  Strike is a dedicated phase — not something that happens informally. The Production Lead does a final "Litter Sweep" of the entire location before the team departs. Equipment left behind and locations not restored are the responsibility of the crew.
</Callout>

## Crew Welfare

Production efficiency drops significantly after five hours of continuous labor. Scheduling breaks is a **technical necessity** — not a nicety — to prevent costly mistakes.

<Tabs items={['Welfare Standards', 'Break Schedule', 'Craft Services']}>
  <Tab value="Welfare Standards">
    **Non-Negotiable Standards:**
    - No more than 5 consecutive hours of work without a break
    - Snacks every 2 hours (water, fruit, light food)
    - Lunch minimum 30 minutes; 60 minutes preferred
    - Shade or shelter available during all outdoor breaks
    - Clear communication on expected wrap time — no surprise overtime
    - Never ask crew to work through a planned break without discussion and agreement
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Break Schedule">
    **Sample 10-Hour Shoot:**

    | Time | Event |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | 6:00 AM | Load-In and gear staging |
    | 6:30 AM | Tech setup |
    | 9:00 AM | **First Break** — 15 min |
    | 9:15 AM | Resume shooting |
    | 12:00 PM | **Lunch** — 60 min |
    | 1:00 PM | Resume shooting |
    | 3:00 PM | **Afternoon Break** — 15 min |
    | 3:15 PM | Resume shooting |
    | 4:30 PM | Strike |
    | 5:30 PM | Wrap |
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Craft Services">
    **Recommended Provisions:**

    **Morning:** Water (always), coffee or tea, light pastries or granola bars

    **Lunch:** Hot and cold options, at least one vegetarian choice, electrolyte drinks

    **Afternoon:** Fresh fruit or protein bars, cold water — critical in Philippine heat

    **Budget:** Roughly ₱200–500 per crew member per full shoot day. Coordinate with the org treasurer for expense reimbursement.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## On-Site Safety

Safety is never sacrificed for creative vision.

**Pre-Shoot Safety Briefing (5 min at Load-In):**
- Identify all emergency exits in the location
- Confirm nearest medical facility (already on Callsheet)
- Review any location-specific hazards (heights, wet floors, live cables)
- Confirm all crew have the Production Lead's number saved

**Gear Safety Rules:**
- Never leave cameras unattended without a crew member in direct line of sight
- All cables taped or covered at crossing points — no exposed runs across walkways
- Sandbags on all C-stands, no exceptions
- Never operate equipment in standing water or heavy rain without proper housing

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="What is the priority if we're running behind schedule?">
    Refer to the Priority column on the Shotlist. Drop 🟢 Bonus first, then 🟡 Nice to Have. Focus entirely on 🔴 Must Have shots. If all Must Haves are captured, you have delivered the minimum viable product.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who is responsible for gear safety on-site?">
    Every crew member is responsible for their immediate station. The Gear Tech leads setup and strike. The Production Lead is responsible for the final inventory check. If any item is missing, wrap does not happen until its status is confirmed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can we keep shooting if talent is exhausted?">
    No. Tired talent produces unusable footage. A 15-minute break consistently yields better takes than pushing through 30 more minutes of diminishing returns. If talent expresses discomfort, stop immediately.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the client SPOC starts directing on-set without going through the Production Lead?">
    Politely redirect: "All creative direction during the shoot goes through [Production Lead name]. This protects your timeline and the quality of the output." Document the interaction. If it continues, pause the shoot for a brief off-camera conversation to re-establish roles.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
