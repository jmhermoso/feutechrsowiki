# Phase 1 — Pre-Pre: Discovery

The discovery phase acts as the **filter** for the entire department. It prevents resource mismanagement by ensuring a project is viable before any technical labor begins. A project that fails discovery should not proceed — no exceptions.

<Callout type="warn" title="This phase is the most important">
  Time spent in discovery saves exponentially more time in every phase that follows. Skipping or rushing this phase is the number one cause of scope creep, on-set chaos, and failed deliverables.
</Callout>

---

## Client Intake

The process begins with a formal request. The production lead must immediately identify a **Single Point of Contact (SPOC)** to prevent "instruction creep" — where multiple voices from the client provide conflicting directions.

| Requirement | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SPOC Identification** | Prevents conflicting revisions and communication bottlenecks. |
| **Core Objective** | Defines if the content is for archival, promotion, or live broadcast. |
| **Target Audience** | Determines tone, pacing, and delivery platform. |
| **Visual References** | 2–3 external links to align on the expected look and feel. |
| **Deadline** | Hard deadline vs. preferred deadline — understanding the difference matters. |

<Steps>
  ### Schedule the intake meeting
  Contact the client within 24 hours of receiving the request.

  ### Identify and document the SPOC
  Record their full name, email, and phone number. Confirm they are the sole decision-maker on the client side.

  ### Ask the key question
  "What does success look like for this project?" — not "What do you want us to make?"

  ### Collect 2–3 reference videos or images
  These should represent the aesthetic and tone the client expects.

  ### Confirm everything in writing
  Core objective, deliverable format, target audience, and hard deadline.
</Steps>

## Scope Definition

With the objective clear, define the **boundaries** of the project. Establishing Creative Freedom vs. Client Mandates at this stage prevents friction during editing.

### The Creative Freedom Matrix

| Zone | Who Decides | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Client Mandate** | Client only | Brand colors, messaging, legal compliance, talent selection |
| **Collaborative** | Lead + Client | Shot composition, narrative structure, pacing, location |
| **Creative Freedom** | Department only | Technical crew, specific equipment, color grading approach |

<Callout type="error" title="Scope Creep Policy">
  Any request that changes the workload by more than **20% after initial approval** must be processed as a **separate project or formal Change Order**.

  **Example:** A 3-minute promo video (8 hrs estimated) with a client add-on of behind-the-scenes B-roll (+4 hrs) = 50% increase. Stop work. Process as a Change Order.
</Callout>

### The Kill Switch Policy

The Production Lead has the authority to **pause the project** if the client:
- Replaces the SPOC without notice
- Demands changes exceeding the 20% scope threshold without a Change Order
- Misses more than one scheduled approval deadline

Document everything in writing.

## Research and Feasibility

Complete both audits before proceeding. A cold assessment of current capabilities prevents overpromising.

<Tabs items={['Hardware Audit', 'Location Audit']}>
  <Tab value="Hardware Audit">
    **Hardware Audit Checklist:**
    - [ ] High-speed cameras available for slow-motion requirements?
    - [ ] Specific lenses (macro, ultra-wide, telephoto) in stock?
    - [ ] Audio gear meeting the client's acoustic requirements?
    - [ ] Lighting kit sufficient for venue size and desired tone?
    - [ ] Backup power (batteries) available?
    - [ ] Storage media (SSD, SD cards) sufficient for expected footage volume?
    - [ ] All gear in working condition? (Test — do not assume.)
    - [ ] Rental budget approved if in-house gear is insufficient?
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Location Audit">
    **Location Audit Checklist:**
    - [ ] **Power:** How many amps available? Where are the circuits?
    - [ ] **Acoustics:** Naturally reverberant or deadened?
    - [ ] **Access:** Elevators? Stairwell dimensions for large gear?
    - [ ] **Environmental:** Weather patterns? Noise permits required?
    - [ ] **Safety:** Trip hazards, heights, or exposure risks?
    - [ ] **Restrictions:** Any areas off-limits or photography restrictions?
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Feasibility Decision Matrix

| Capability Gap | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| Missing equipment, rental budget available | Proceed — include rental costs in project brief |
| Missing equipment, no rental budget | Offer a lower technical tier with clear trade-offs |
| Location is inaccessible or restricted | Propose alternative location or cancel |
| Timeline is impossible given crew availability | Negotiate deadline extension before committing |

## Pre-Pre Deliverables Checklist

Before this phase is **closed**, the following must exist in writing:

- [ ] Intake form completed with SPOC documented
- [ ] Core objective confirmed in writing
- [ ] Creative Freedom Matrix completed
- [ ] Hardware Audit completed
- [ ] Location Audit completed (if applicable)
- [ ] Feasibility decision documented — Proceed / Proceed with caveats / Do not proceed

<Callout type="info" title="Phase Gate">
  Do not begin Pre-Production until all Pre-Pre deliverables are approved by the Production Lead and Department Head.
</Callout>

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="How do we handle a client who refuses to provide a SPOC?">
    Inform the client that without a single point of contact, the production timeline will be extended to account for conflicting revisions and the Kill Switch policy will be strictly enforced. Document this conversation in writing. If they still refuse, escalate to the Department Head before proceeding.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the request exceeds our equipment's capabilities?">
    Flag this in the feasibility report. The project may only proceed if the client provides budget for rentals or explicitly accepts a lower technical tier. Always present both options in writing. Never overpromise capabilities you cannot deliver.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who owns the feasibility decision?">
    The Production Lead makes the final call. If uncertain, escalate to the Department Head before communicating the decision to the client.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can a project skip discovery if we've worked with the client before?">
    No. Repeat clients still require a brief intake to confirm the scope of the current project. Previous relationships do not eliminate the risk of scope creep or changing requirements. A 15-minute intake call is always worth it.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
