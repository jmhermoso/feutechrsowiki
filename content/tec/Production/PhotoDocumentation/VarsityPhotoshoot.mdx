# Varsity Photoshoot

The Varsity Photoshoot is a structured, collaborative production conducted in coordination with Esports Management. Its purpose is to produce a complete set of official photos for each varsity player — covering standard poses for competitive and publication use, as well as creative shots for birthday posts and general promotional material.

Productions officers are responsible for shoot logistics, on-site direction, and post-shoot file management. Esports Management, through the varsity managers, handles player scheduling and coordination.

<Callout type="info" title="Collaboration Checkpoint">
  This shoot cannot proceed without confirmation from the varsity managers on player availability. Productions must not set a venue or time unilaterally — scheduling is a joint decision with Esports Management.
</Callout>

---

## Pre-Shoot Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Coordinate with Esports Management

    Initiate the scheduling process by reaching out to the relevant varsity managers:

    - Identify which teams are included in the shoot (confirm with Productions lead)
    - Share a proposed date range and venue options with the varsity managers
    - Request player availability windows from each manager — do not assume all players are available on the same day
    - Confirm the finalized schedule in writing (group chat or message thread) before booking the venue

    <Callout type="warn" title="Player Availability is Non-Negotiable">
      If a player cannot make the scheduled date, coordinate a separate slot — do not proceed without them. Incomplete team photo sets create gaps in publication materials.
    </Callout>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Book the Venue and Time

    Once availability is confirmed:

    - Book the venue with sufficient time per player (recommended: **15–20 minutes per player** for a full pose set)
    - Account for setup and teardown time (minimum 30 minutes before first player, 20 minutes after last)
    - Share the finalized schedule with Esports Management and the Productions lead
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Prepare Equipment and Setup

    Before shoot day, prepare the following:

    - Camera body and lenses appropriate for indoor/studio shooting (standard: 50mm or 85mm prime for portrait work)
    - Lighting setup — confirm with the Productions lead what equipment is available
    - Backdrop — confirm color and size with Creatives; standard recommendation is a seamless paper or fabric backdrop, matte finish only for on-camera use
    - Reference sheet — print or load the pose list so the photographer and players can reference it on-site
    - Confirm the Creatives department has prepared any overlays or templates that will be applied to final outputs
  </Step>
</Steps>

## On-Site Shoot Guide

### Pose Categories

Each player must complete three categories of photos. Work through them in order to maintain a consistent shoot pace.

<Tabs items={['Standard Poses', 'Creative Poses']}>
  <Tab value="Standard Poses">
    Standard poses are taken from two angles for each position.

    **Angles:**
    - **Frontal** — player faces directly toward the camera
    - **Side** — player turns 45° to 90° to the camera (either side; confirm with Creatives for consistency across the team)

    **Poses per angle:**

    | Pose | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Arms Crossed** | Player crosses arms at chest level. Expression: composed, confident. Commonly used for roster cards and competitive profiles. |
    | **Arms to the Side** | Player stands with arms relaxed at their sides. Expression: neutral to confident. Used as a clean, versatile baseline shot. |

    > Shoot both angles for each pose — a full standard set per player is **4 shots minimum** (2 poses × 2 angles). Capture multiple frames per pose and select the best during culling.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Creative Poses">
    Creative poses are used for birthday posts, promotional graphics, and general publication material. These are taken frontally unless Creatives specifies otherwise.

    | Pose | Description | Primary Use |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Creative Pose** | Open-ended expressive pose — player may suggest their own or be guided by the photographer. Should feel natural and personality-driven, not stiff. | General publications, feature graphics |
    | **Victory Pose** | Player expresses a win reaction — raised fist, pointing, arms up, or team-specific celebration gesture. Energy should read clearly on camera. | Post-win announcements, hype content |
    | **Defeat Pose** | Player expresses a loss reaction — head down, hands on head, or a somber look into the camera. Must be dignified, not mocking. | Reflection posts, narrative content |

    <Callout type="info" title="Directing Creative Poses">
      Brief the player before the shoot on what each creative pose is for. Give them a moment to get comfortable before shooting. For victory and defeat poses, a short verbal prompt (e.g. "you just won a finals match" or "last game of the season") helps players find authentic expressions.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Shoot Order and Pacing

- Work through one player's full set before moving to the next — do not mix players' sessions
- Recommended order per player: **Standard Poses → Creative Poses**
- Allow a short break between players to review selects and clear mental space
- If a player is late, move to the next available player and return to the late player at the end of the session

## Post-Shoot File Management

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Immediate File Transfer

    As soon as the shoot ends:

    - Transfer all raw files from the memory card to the local workstation
    - Do not reformat the memory card until the transfer is verified complete
    - Create a shoot folder using the naming convention: `[TeamName]_Varsity_[YYYY-MM-DD]` (e.g. `MLBB_Varsity_2025-08-14`)
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Culling and Tagging

    Cull each player's photos into organized subfolders:

    - Create a subfolder per player named by their IGN or full name — confirm naming convention with Esports Management
    - Within each player's folder, sort into: `RAW_Selects` and `RAW_Rejects`
    - Select the best frame per pose per angle — minimum 1 select per required shot, up to 3 selects per pose for Creatives to choose from
    - Flag or label selects clearly before export
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Export for Creatives

    Export selects for overlay work:

    - Export at full resolution, `.jpg` or `.tiff` per Creatives' specifications
    - Place exports in an `Exports_ForCreatives` subfolder within each player's folder
    - Notify the Creatives department that files are ready and share the drive link
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Archive to Shared Drive

    Upload the complete shoot folder to the shared drive, replicating the structure below exactly:

    <Files>
      <Folder name="MLBB_Varsity_2025-08-14" defaultOpen>
        <Folder name="PlayerIGN_01">
          <Folder name="RAW_Selects">

            <File name="MLBB_01_side_crossed_001.jpg" />
            <File name="MLBB_01_creative_victory_001.jpg" />
          </Folder>
          <Folder name="RAW_Rejects">
            <File name="MLBB_01_frontal_crossed_002.jpg" />
          </Folder>
          <Folder name="Exports_ForCreatives">
            <File name="MLBB_01_frontal_crossed_FINAL.jpg" />
            <File name="MLBB_01_creative_victory_FINAL.jpg" />
          </Folder>
        </Folder>
        <Folder name="PlayerIGN_02">
          <File name="..." />
        </Folder>
      </Folder>
    </Files>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Confirm Completion with Esports Management

    Once the archive is uploaded and Creatives have been notified:

    - Send a completion message to the varsity managers confirming the shoot is done and files are archived
    - Note any players who were absent and need a reschedule
    - Log the shoot in the Productions records (date, team, number of players completed, outstanding reschedules)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## File Naming Convention

All exported files must follow this naming structure:

```
[TeamCode]_[PlayerIGN]_[Angle]_[Pose]_[Sequence].jpg
```

| Token | Example Values |
|---|---|
| `TeamCode` | `MLBB`, `VALORANT`, `LOL` |
| `PlayerIGN` | Player's in-game name, no spaces (use underscores) |
| `Angle` | `frontal`, `side` |
| `Pose` | `crossed`, `sidesarms`, `creative`, `victory`, `defeat` |
| `Sequence` | `001`, `002`, `003` |

**Example:** `MLBB_ShadowFang_frontal_crossed_001.jpg`

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="What if a player misses their scheduled slot?">
    Coordinate with the varsity manager to reschedule as soon as possible. Document the absence in your post-shoot report. Do not leave a player's folder empty in the archive — create a placeholder folder with a note file indicating the reschedule is pending.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How many selects should be kept per pose?">
    Keep a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 3 selects per required pose per player. The final selection for overlay work is made by Creatives — your job is to provide quality options, not make the final cut.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can players suggest their own creative poses?">
    Yes, and it is encouraged — player-driven creative poses tend to look more natural and authentic. However, ensure the shot is still clean, well-lit, and usable as publication material. If the player's suggested pose does not translate well on camera, guide them toward an alternative.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What backdrop color should be used?">
    Confirm with Creatives before the shoot, as the backdrop color must complement the overlay templates. If no direction is given, default to a neutral (white, grey, or black) matte-finish backdrop. Avoid glossy surfaces that create glare on camera.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who is responsible for player briefing on the day of the shoot?">
    The Productions officer on-site is responsible for briefing players on the pose categories and what each shot will be used for. Varsity managers may assist in relaying instructions, but the photographer leads the session.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens after Creatives finishes the overlays?">
    Creatives will return finished files to the shared drive or notify Productions directly. Productions coordinates with Publications for deployment. Varsity photo deployment (e.g. roster reveals, birthday posts) is scheduled by Publications — confirm the handoff process with them separately.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
