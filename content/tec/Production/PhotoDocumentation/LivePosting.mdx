# Live Posting

Live posting is the process of capturing photos during an event, editing them with branded overlays supplied by the Creatives department, and publishing them through the Publications department — all within a tight turnaround window. The goal is timely, on-brand visual coverage that reflects the event in real time.

<Callout type="warn" title="Coordination Required">
  Do not begin editing or posting without confirming the active overlay set with Creatives and the posting schedule or approval chain with Publications. Posting unbranded or unapproved photos is not permitted.
</Callout>

---

## Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Pre-Event Preparation

    Before the event begins, complete the following:

    - Confirm the event details (date, venue, time) with the Productions lead
    - Obtain the overlay template package from the Creatives department — verify it is the correct version for the event
    - Confirm with Publications the posting schedule, platform targets (e.g. Facebook, Instagram), and the approval process for go-live
    - Charge all camera equipment and clear sufficient storage space on memory cards
    - Conduct a brief test shot at the venue to assess lighting conditions and adjust settings accordingly
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Photo Capture (On-Site)

    During the event, the photographer is responsible for:

    - Prioritizing high-impact moments: key plays, player reactions, team interactions, and crowd shots
    - Shooting in the highest quality format available; RAW + JPEG is preferred if simultaneous editing is planned
    - Flagging selects in-camera (using star ratings or color labels where supported) to speed up culling later
    - Maintaining awareness of the posting window — identify and cull selects while the event is still ongoing if possible

    <Callout type="info" title="Shot Priority">
      Focus on faces, action, and emotion. Clean backgrounds and sharp focus on the subject are non-negotiable for posted photos. Blurry or obscured subject shots should not be posted regardless of the moment.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Culling and Selection

    After capture, select photos for posting:

    - Transfer files to the editing workstation immediately after or during a break in the event
    - Cull to the best **3–5 selects per posting batch** — quality over quantity
    - Selects must have: sharp subject focus, good exposure (not recoverable only in post), clear subject visibility, and no distracting obstructions
    - Rejected files should be kept in the raw dump folder — do not delete on-site
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Editing with Overlays

    Apply the overlay template provided by Creatives:

    <Tabs items={['Adobe Photoshop', 'Adobe Lightroom', 'Canva / Alternative']}>
      <Tab value="Adobe Photoshop">
        1. Open the select photo as the base layer
        2. Place the overlay file (`.psd` or `.png`) as a Smart Object above the photo layer
        3. Adjust the photo position/scale within the overlay's designated safe zone
        4. Flatten and export as `.jpg` at **72 dpi, sRGB**, sized to the platform spec provided by Publications
        5. Name the file following the convention: `[EventCode]_[Sequence]_posted.jpg` (e.g. `MLBB_001_posted.jpg`)
      </Tab>
      <Tab value="Adobe Lightroom">
        1. Apply basic exposure and color corrections in the Develop module
        2. Export the corrected photo as a `.png` or `.jpg`
        3. Open in Photoshop or Canva to apply the overlay layer
        4. Export final file per naming convention above
      </Tab>
      <Tab value="Canva / Alternative">
        1. Upload the select photo to the approved Canva template provided by Creatives
        2. Replace the placeholder image with your select, adjusting position within the safe zone
        3. Download as `.jpg` (high quality) or `.png` per Publications' platform requirements
        4. Rename the downloaded file per naming convention above
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    <Callout type="warn" title="Do Not Alter the Overlay">
      The overlay template must not be modified — no repositioning of logos, changing colors, or removing any branded element. If the overlay does not fit a particular photo, select a different photo or escalate to Creatives.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Submission to Publications

    Once edited files are ready:

    - Upload final files to the designated shared drive folder for Publications (confirm path before the event)
    - Notify the Publications officer-in-charge via the agreed channel (e.g. group chat, direct message) that files are ready for review
    - Include the following in your message: event name, number of photos submitted, and any notes on specific shots (e.g. "Photo 3 is the highlight moment — recommended for story")
    - Wait for Publications' confirmation or request for revision before considering the batch complete
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Post-Event File Management

    After the event and once all posts are live:

    - Transfer all raw files (unedited selects and rejects) to the raw archive folder on the shared drive
    - Transfer all edited/posted files to the posted archive folder
    - Label the folder with the event name and date: `[EventName]_[YYYY-MM-DD]`
    - Confirm with the Productions lead that the archive is complete
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Turnaround Time Standards

| Stage | Target Time |
|---|---|
| First batch of selects culled | Within 30 minutes of event start |
| First edited batch submitted to Publications | Within 1 hour of event start |
| Full post-event archive uploaded | Within 24 hours after event end |

## FAQ

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="What if I don't receive the overlay before the event?">
    Escalate immediately to the Creatives department and copy the Productions lead. Do not shoot without confirming you will have the overlay — if it cannot be provided in time, the Productions lead decides whether to proceed or delay live posting.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How many photos should be submitted per posting batch?">
    Standard is 3–5 photos per batch. Publications may request more or fewer depending on the platform and event type. Confirm the expected volume during pre-event coordination.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if Publications requests a revision on an edited photo?">
    Re-edit the specific photo based on the feedback and re-upload to the shared folder. Notify Publications again once the revision is ready. Do not replace files without informing them.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I post directly without going through Publications?">
    No. All live posts must go through the Publications department. Productions handles capture and editing; Publications handles distribution and scheduling. This ensures consistency and avoids duplicate or conflicting posts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to photos that were not selected for posting?">
    All non-posted photos from the shoot must still be archived in the raw dump folder. They may be used later for recap posts, internal documentation, or future publication materials.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What file format and resolution should exported photos be?">
    Export at 72 dpi in sRGB color space as .jpg for social media. Confirm the exact pixel dimensions with Publications before the event, as platform specifications may vary (e.g. Facebook vs. Instagram feed vs. story).
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
